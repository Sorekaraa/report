Joint Contract Mechanism (Electricity/Gas)

By aggregating the electricity and gas consumption of multiple local businesses, we conduct large-scale bidding/auctions with energy retailers. This maximizes negotiation power, leading to significant reductions in utility costs and promoting the use of **renewable energy (SDGs 7 & 13)**.

Expected Effects of Joint Contract

Indicator Definition Result/Target Conditions
Discount Rate (incl. Renewables) Reduction rate in joint electricity contract, including renewable energy. Average 7–10% discount Electricity usage must exceed a certain level. Multiple suppliers compete through bidding/auction.
Example Savings Example of actual savings from switching to a joint electricity contract. Average 9% annual reduction (approx. ¥10,000) observed in cases. The higher the electricity usage, the greater the absolute monetary saving.
Trend of Renewable Energy Surcharge The proportion of the surcharge in commercial electricity bill components. Surcharge accounts for approx. 6% Requires review of rate plans or selection of non-fossil fuel certificate-backed electricity.

Joint Contract Implementation Steps

  • 1. Current Situation Analysis and Data Collection Collect the past 12–24 months of electricity usage and contract terms for each store.
  • 2. Grouping and Demand Presentation Group stores by demand pattern (e.g., daytime usage type / nighttime usage type) and consolidate the capacity of multiple stores.
  • 3. Contract Negotiation and Method Determination Adopt a **reverse auction method** to create competition among multiple power companies. Decide on fixed price / market-linked plan.
  • 4. Contract Execution and Monitoring Sign contracts, notify existing power companies of the switch, and continuously monitor usage and charges after the switch.
