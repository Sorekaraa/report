Problem and Solution Overview

The shortage of non-regular employees in the restaurant industry reaches 70%, with challenges in handling sudden absences and peak hours. To address this, we will build a talent sharing platform based on "Common Training" and a "Qualification System."

1. Introduction of Common Training Program (SDG 8 & 9)

  • Common Training: Basic training in food hygiene, customer service, POS operation, and **standard Konamon recipes** acquired via e-learning.
  • Effect: Achieves standardization of training, contributing to a reduction in training costs (**approx. 30% reduction** in other case studies).

2. Introduction of "Konamon Part-time Qualification" System (SDG 8 & 11)

  • Qualification System: Evaluation combining written exams (food hygiene, service, POS operation) and practical exams (cooking demonstration, role-play, register operation) to **certify job-ready personnel**.
  • Effect: Qualified individuals receive incentives such as higher hourly wages. Improves motivation and retention rates.

3. Establishment of a Talent Sharing Platform

Qualified personnel are shared among cooperating local businesses for peak hours or staff absences. An IT system optimizes shift management and matching.

Benefits

Stakeholder Benefit SDGs Contribution
Businesses Cost reduction, immediate staffing during peak times, stable labor supply. SDG 8, 9
Employees Higher wages, skill certification, increased job mobility within the region. SDG 8
Region Improved service quality, creation of a shared talent pool, and a more resilient local economy. SDG 11
