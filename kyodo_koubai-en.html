Joint Purchasing Mechanism

By aggregating the purchasing volume of multiple local businesses, we gain strong negotiation power with suppliers, leading to a reduction in raw material unit costs and ensuring stable procurement. This directly contributes to SDG 8 (Economic Growth).

Expected Effects of Joint Purchasing

Item Average Price Reduction Target Notes
Main Flours 5-10% High-volume purchasing from domestic or bulk importers.
Eggs/Meat/Cabbage 3-5% Contracts with local producers for stable supply and freshness.
Logistics Costs 10-15% Consolidating multiple small-lot deliveries into one large delivery.

Legal Entity Options for Cooperative Groups

The choice of legal entity affects the availability of subsidies and flexibility in profit distribution. We recommend forming a Business Cooperative (Jigyō Kyōdō Kumiai) for long-term stability.

Business Cooperative General Incorporated Association LLP (Limited Liability Partnership)
Benefit Eligible for subsidies and regulatory compliance Quick and flexible establishment Ideal for small-scale trials
Drawback Subject to administrative approval and oversight No profit distribution possible Limited credibility

Implementation Steps

  • 1. Initial Survey Phase (1-2 months) Select target region, gather number of stores (target 5-20), and interview for common purchase items, volume/frequency, current unit costs, and logistics.
  • 2. Trial Order Phase (3-4 months) Select 5-10 common items (flour, cabbage, oil, etc.), decide on a joint ordering schedule, and start logistics arrangements.
  • 3. Evaluation Phase (1 month after trial) Compare reduction amount, unit cost decrease rate, and delivery costs using data, and identify operational issues.
  • 4. Full Implementation Phase Expand item list (e.g., Halal certified ingredients), link with local specialties, and conduct regular reviews of purchase prices and logistics costs.
